Travel Demand Management (TDM)

Travel Demand Management (TDM) is a coordinated package of strategies that reduces and reshapes background travel demand so that the roadway, transit, and active transportation networks can absorb World Cup trips while maintaining a level of service similar to a typical day.

How TDM Works

  • Work From Home – Telecommuting eliminates the daily commute trip entirely, providing the single largest lever for reducing peak period vehicle demand.
  • Flexible Timing – Staggering start and end times spreads demand across the day, reducing the sharp spike that creates gridlock during peak windows.
  • Avoid Busy Areas – Routing around congestion hotspots like Downtown, NRG Stadium, and the Galleria distributes vehicle loads more evenly across the network.
  • Alternative Modes – Public transit, carpooling, biking, and walking move more people per lane — reducing vehicle trips while maintaining overall mobility.
  • Plan Ahead – Advance trip planning using real-time data allows travelers to choose optimal routes, modes, and departure times.
  • Make Fewer Trips – Combining errands, trip chaining, and online alternatives reduce the total number of vehicle trips generated.

Mobility in the Region

Houston Today
  • One of the most developed highway transportation systems in the world.
  • Serves 2.84 million households (8-county estimate for 2026).
  • 27.3 million person trips and 21.7 million vehicle trips on a typical weekday.
  • 200 million vehicle miles traveled daily.
  • High recurring congestion on I-610, BW 8, I-10, I-45, I-69, SH 288, US 290 & SH 6.
FIFA World Cup Impact
  • Thousands of international and domestic visitors arriving, staying, and departing during the event.
  • Increased traffic in and around NRG Stadium, Fan Fest, airports and tourist hotspots (Downtown, Galleria, NASA).
  • High demand for hotels, restaurants, flights, transit, and rideshare.
  • World Cup demand overlaps directly with the region's highest congestion areas.

Why TDM

As any Houstonian knows, it does not take much for traffic to break down during peak hours on key roadways on a typical day. During FIFA World Cup, an already saturated transportation system will experience significant additional demand. Effective TDM can help reduce background traffic so the system handles World Cup demand at a similar level of service as a typical day.

TDM Benefits
  • Reduce Peak Period Congestion – Lowers vehicle demand during both peak and peak-shoulder periods, creating measurable relief on the most congested corridors.
  • Improve Travel Time Reliability – Especially critical before game start (9:00–11:00 AM) and after game end (2:00–4:00 PM) when most event trips occur.
  • Reduce Congestion at Fan Fest – Decreases vehicle trips and parking pressure in Downtown and EaDo during Fan Festival activities.
  • Buffer for Non-Recurring Events – Creates headroom to absorb incidents, accidents, and weather-related disruptions.

Recommended TDM Strategy

  1. Keep the excitement alive. Messaging must maintain the thrill of the World Cup — never create panic or anxiety about travel conditions.
  2. Communication is the key. Keep people informed so they can make good decisions, with timely information to nudge behavior in the right direction.
  3. Use personas to simplify. Example: “If you are a resident in Katy who wants to visit Fan Fest, here is what you need to know.”
  4. Target significant trip reduction. Effective TDM needs especially strong results inside and around I-610, with a goal of 5–10% reduction in total daily trips.
  5. Smart commute options. Coordinate with Game Plan — Commute Solutions for employer engagement and carpool/transit incentive programs.
Target Groups for TDM Outreach
  • Flexible Residents & Workers – Can adjust travel times or routes based on accurate, timely information.
  • Event-Related Travellers – Visitors and local participants generating extra demand; can be guided to optimal arrival windows and modes.
  • Fixed Schedule Commuters – Cannot change or delay trips; benefit from route guidance and transit alternatives.
  • Essential Service Providers – Must travel to deliver critical services; require priority routing and real-time incident information.
